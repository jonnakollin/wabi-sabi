In Zen philosophy, there are seven aesthetic principles in achieving wabi-sabi:

Kanso — simplicity 

Fukinsei — asymmetry or irregularity

Shibumi — beauty in the understated

Shizen — naturalness without pretense

Yugen — subtle grace

Datsuzoku — freeness

Seijaku — tranquility
